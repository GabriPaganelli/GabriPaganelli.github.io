Projects

This study analyzed the dynamic relationships between natural gas prices, renewable energy production, and the Italian national electricity price (PUN) over 2021–2025. Using a combination of univariate SARIMA, VAR, transfer function, and cointegration models, we identified short- and long-term interactions, highlighting the dominant role of gas in price formation, and provided predictive evidence that TFM_Gas is superior for short- to medium-term forecasting.

I developed and evaluated a sequence of Bayesian mixture models to classify high-energy neutrino events as astrophysical or atmospheric, combining spatial and energetic information. Models included joint spatial-energetic frameworks, spatial-weighted implementations, and energy-only variants with posterior spatial reweighting. Severe spectral degeneracy limited discrimination, but the workflow demonstrated a coherent Bayesian approach for large-scale event classification.

Our experiments demonstrate that explicitly modeling temporal structure in band-limited LFP activity with a GRU-Transformer yields significantly better decoding of visual stimuli than a linear classifier trained on flattened power features. Improvements were consistent across multiple sessions, conditions, and class-imbalanced datasets, highlighting the importance of temporal dynamics in neural decoding.

This project analyzed the Pantheon dataset, modeling over 11,000 historical figures as a directed network of interactions via Wikipedia hyperlinks, by integrating exploratory analysis (spatio-temporal distributions, centrality metrics) and inferential modeling with ERGMs on subnetworks. It identified data biases and model limitations (ERGMs degeneracy, computational complexity for large networks).
